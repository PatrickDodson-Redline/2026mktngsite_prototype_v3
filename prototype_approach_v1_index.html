Approach

Why companies choose Redline Growth.

AI-native GTM tools and approaches are maturing rapidly. The knowledge, experience, and skills to architect and deploy them is still a challenge for many. Redline addresses this gap for clients through discrete projects and ongoing managed services.

A model designed for rapidly-evolving environments

Several ways to address your pipeline growth gap. Many with gaps of their own.

Understanding the different models of GTM support helps in determining what you get from each, and what is still left to account for.

GTM Agencies
Runs outbound sequences on your behalf. The campaigns produce meetings, but the agency holds the capability. When the engagement ends, so does the benefit.
RevOps Consultancies
Designs and implements GTM systems, then hands them off. The system is yours, but so is the ongoing operation, improvement, and innovation.
Data / Intent Platforms
Gives you access to data but not actionable insights. Translating the signals they offer into the insights you truly need to produce rep-ready output is still your responsibility (and challenge).
Managed Intelligence Services Firms
Companies that partner with clients to build, deploy, and operate customized signal-enriched solutions. Clients own the data, the partner operates the engine as a managed service. The output is specific to the client’s business and ICP, and routed to reps through existing tools and systems.

This is Redline Growth.

GTM Intelligence Maturity Model

Where is your organization today?

Most B2B SaaS companies sit between Stage 1 and Stage 3.
The gap to Stage 4 is what Redline closes.

Stage 1
Reactive
Tools are present. Signal capture is inconsistent. Reps do manual research because nothing connects the stack into a coordinated output.
Stage 2
Operationalized
Tools are integrated at a basic level. Outbound sequences run. The intelligence layer that tells reps which account to prioritize and why was never built.
Stage 3
Integrated
The stack is connected. Data flows between tools. But the intent signals are commodity-grade: the same few vendors offer their data to any of your competitors.
Stage 4
Compounding
Customized intelligence at both the account and contact-level, specific to your ICP and market. Signals that competitors cannot purchase from a system that gets more accurate over time.

Redline engagements establish where your organization sits today, then build directly to Stage 4 capability.

How to know when there is a strong fit

Companies that benefit the most from Redline share a few key characteristics.

01

A B2B selling motion with a defined ICP and enough deal volume for an intelligence investment to pay off.

02

They want intelligence delivered to their reps, not assembled by them. They have seen what happens when reps are handed a better tool and expected to extract value on their own.

03

They want a partner managing the intelligence engine. Not another platform to configure and operate internally. Some want Redline to run it indefinitely. Others want Redline to build and operate it initially, then transfer operations in-house. Both paths are designed to succeed.

How we engage

Two engagement formats. Both produce actionable intelligence for your team.

Redline works in two formats. Project-based engagements produce a defined intelligence asset on a fixed scope and timeline. Subscription engagements operate the live engine that produces sustained pipeline growth through your reps’ existing workflows. Many client relationships start with a project to validate the methodology and convert to subscription once the value is proven.

Project engagement
Defined intelligence asset, fixed scope.

A time-bound piece of work producing a specific intelligence deliverable. The work product is yours to use as a point-in-time intelligence asset inside your CRM, outbound platform, or sales workflow. Scoped and priced per engagement.

Service modes
Detect. Identify the right accounts, contacts, and signals.
Translate. Turn raw signals into intelligence reps can act on.
Advisory. Evaluate and recommend on motions, systems, and strategies you operate internally.
Typical durations
Tier 1: two to four weeks. Focused engagement with one defined deliverable.

Tier 2: four to ten weeks. Multi-phase engagement producing a connected intelligence asset.
Subscription engagement
Ongoing intelligence engine, sustained rep enablement.

The live engine operating inside your team’s workflow. Recurring dossier production through the tools your reps already use. Signal architecture maintained and tuned throughout the engagement lifecycle.

Pilot phase
Four to six weeks to stand up the engine. Four to six months for measurable pipeline growth.
Activation
Dedicated GTM engineer. Weekly strategy meetings, ongoing signal optimization and engine refinement. Four-to-six-month pilot with option to continue based on pilot results.
Long-term paths
Ongoing managed service: Redline operates the engine indefinitely.

Transition to internal: Redline operates initially, then progressively transfers execution to your team as internal capability grows. The transition is measured against demonstrated internal capability, not a calendar date.

Client involvement

Discovery requires three to four hours of your sales leadership’s time across one to two sessions. You bring the market knowledge. Redline builds the architecture. Ongoing involvement is a weekly thirty-to-forty-five minute strategy meeting with the GTM engineer. Reps use the dossiers and flag when a dossier misses the mark, which directly improves scoring accuracy. No dedicated project manager required. No new platform to learn.

FAQ

The questions we hear most often. Answered directly.

If the answer you need is not here, the scoping conversation is the right place to get it.

Fit and qualification
How do I know if Redline is the right fit?
Two gates. First, a B2B selling motion with a defined ICP. Second, deal volume and average deal size that justify the investment (the calculator can model that). If both are met, the question is which engagement format fits. Smaller teams or specific intelligence needs often start with a project. Larger teams with ongoing pipeline goals point toward a subscription. Companies that prefer a fully self-serve platform with no managed component are not a fit for the subscription model, but may still benefit from project work.
Do I need to have Clay already?
No. If you have Clay, Redline builds inside your environment. If you do not, Redline operates from its own instance. The intelligence is identical. See the Clay section on the Capabilities page for details on both paths.
We are a Series B company with fewer than ten reps. Is Redline too early?
Not necessarily. Run is designed for five to fifteen reps, and the use case is well established at Series B. The question is whether the economics work relative to your deal size and capacity. Run the calculator to get a directional answer.
We are at Mid-Market scale. Is Redline designed for organizations our size?
Yes. Blast Off is designed for thirty to fifty reps. At that scale the problem is typically vendor sprawl and fragmented signal rather than missing infrastructure, and the engagement starts with an audit of your existing stack. Orbit handles organizations above fifty reps or with multi-function requirements.
The build and timeline
How long does it take to see results?
The build phase runs four to six weeks: discovery, signal taxonomy, ICP calibration, and the first dossiers in front of reps. Measurable pipeline impact visible within four to six months. The first dossiers produce conversations before that window, but a meaningful, measurable improvement requires two to three months at full engine capacity.
Can we start with a subset of accounts?
Yes, and that is often how the engagement starts. The pilot covers a scoped account set, typically your highest-priority tier. The signal taxonomy and scoring model are architected to scale where appropriate.
What does our team need to do during the build phase?
Discovery requires three to four hours of sales leadership time across one to two sessions. After that, the GTM engineer handles the build. Your team reviews the draft signal taxonomy before it goes live and confirms dossier routing is configured correctly.
Does Redline replace our existing stack?
It is not required but may create that opportunity. The intelligence engine layers on top of your existing stack. It works with the CRM, sequence tool, and enrichment vendors you already use. In some engagements the pilot reveals point-solution data or intent tools producing redundancy or noise without adding signal, and those become consolidation candidates. Redline does not displace your CRM or outbound platform. It makes them more effective.
The intelligence itself
How is this different from buying ZoomInfo or a similar intent data platform?
Intent data platforms sell category-level data: which companies in your segment are researching a category of solution. They sell the same feed to every company in your category. Custom signal intelligence is structurally different. It is built from collaborative discovery with your team, calibrated to the specific accounts and behaviors that predict purchases in your market. Competitors cannot purchase it from a data vendor.
Can we just build this in Clay ourselves?
Sure, but know going in that it requires platform familiarity and GTM engineers to optimize the investment beyond the basic infrastructure-layer functionality. Most savvy RevOps teams can build a basic outbound workflow on the platform. The intelligence layer is the greater challenge: signal taxonomy, enrichment waterfall architecture, scoring models, ICP calibration, AI context generation. Then there is the ongoing operation of calibrating against real pipeline outcomes and refining as the market shifts. Redline does this work every day across multiple clients, which is why our builds compress to four to six weeks and generate measurable pipeline growth in four to six months.
What signals does the engine monitor?
Signal selection is specific to each engagement and determined during discovery. Common categories include hiring patterns, leadership transitions, technology adoption events, competitive displacement signals, budget cycle indicators, earnings call language, and social content patterns from key decision-makers.
Can we see an example of the intelligence output?
Contact Redline directly for a walkthrough of the intelligence output format options. The discovery and scoping conversations include examples calibrated to your segment and use case.
Engagement model and pricing
What is the difference between a project and a subscription?
A project produces a defined intelligence deliverable on a fixed scope and timeline. A subscription operates the live engine on a recurring basis. Projects typically run two to ten weeks. Subscriptions run multi-month with a dedicated GTM engineer. Many client relationships start as a project and convert to subscription once the value is proven.
What is included in the subscription?
The subscription includes the full intelligence engine build: discovery, signal taxonomy, ICP calibration, dossier routing, and ongoing dossier production. It also includes the dedicated GTM engineer, weekly strategy meetings, and ongoing signal optimization. Project work (integration builds, data cleansing, advisory sprints, CRM remediation) is scoped and priced separately when the engagement reveals a need.
What is the difference between managed service and transition to internal?
Managed service means Redline operates the engine with no defined endpoint. Transition to internal means Redline operates initially, then progressively transfers execution to your team. The transition is measured against demonstrated internal capability and capacity, not a calendar date. Both paths are fully supported.
Data, security, and vendor dependency
How does Redline handle client data?
Clay maintains commercial agreements with all data providers in its enrichment network, addressing data sourcing compliance at the platform level. When Redline operates from a client’s existing Clay workspace, all data stays in the client’s environment under their existing terms. When Redline operates from its own instance, client data is segregated, access is restricted to the engagement team, and data handling terms are documented in the engagement agreement. At engagement close, client data is either migrated to the client’s own environment or deleted, per the client’s preference.
Do you have access to our CRM data?
Discovery requires shared context about your ICP and historical pipeline. Integration with your CRM for dossier routing typically involves an API connection configured with your RevOps lead. Redline does not store CRM records independently. Data handling is confirmed during discovery and documented in engagement terms.
What happens if we want to end the engagement?
If the intelligence was built inside your Clay instance, it is yours. No migration needed. If Redline’s instance was used, the full architecture is documented and Redline supports migration to your own environment. The signal taxonomy, ICP calibration, and scoring logic are documented and transferable regardless of path.
We want to build internal capability eventually.
That is what the transition-to-internal path is designed for. Redline builds and operates the engine, progressively documents the architecture, trains the internal team, and transfers operational responsibility as capability grows. Name that priority in the scoping conversation and the engagement will be structured around it.
Company and credibility
Redline is a newer firm. How do we know this will work?
Redline holds Advanced Artisan Partner status in Clay’s Solutions Partner Program. The signal detection, enrichment, and AI context generation Redline deploys run at scale every day, and we have current and past client successes across all segments served. Select outcomes are documented on the About page. Contact Redline directly for a walkthrough of experience and output relevant to your segment.
Do you work with companies outside the B2B SaaS segments listed?
Primarily no. The engagement model is optimized for B2B selling motions with a defined ICP and a repeatable outbound motion. If your organization is outside those segments but has a similar structure, a scoping conversation is the right way to assess whether the model translates.
Does Redline run outbound campaigns for clients?
Yes. Redline designs and executes outbound motions on clients’ behalf. The difference from traditional cold outbound is that every outreach campaign is signal-enriched before it goes out. A contact receives an email because a specific trigger event created a genuine opening, not because an account appeared on a list.
